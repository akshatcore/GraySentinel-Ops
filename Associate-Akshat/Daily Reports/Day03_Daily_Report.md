## Key Learnings Today

- The **Socratic method** is not just a teaching philosophy — it's a practical efficiency tool. Students who are guided to the answer retain it. Students given direct answers come back with the same question.
- **Evidence-first culture** is the single biggest process improvement possible in the doubt group. The doubt template idea (requiring error message + command + what was tried) would cut average resolution time significantly.
- **VM network adapter mode** is the hidden root cause of at least 30% of "Kali not working" questions. It should be a pinned FAQ before students even start labs.
- Rubber duck debugging is underutilised. It should be proactively encouraged in onboarding, not just introduced when a student is stuck.

---

## Blockers / Observations

- The current doubt tracking has no systematic pipeline — doubts resolved in WhatsApp do not automatically enter the knowledge base. This creates repeated work for the team.
- Some students post doubts without reading the group's pinned resources. A pinned FAQ index (even a simple numbered list) would reduce this.
- No standardised format for evidence submission. Asciinema as a standard tool would improve this immediately.

---

## Suggestions Raised to Mentor
